Discover my Tech Journey at B-CU: My First Job in America

At Bethune-Cookman University, basking in the radiant sunshine of Daytona Beach, FL, I embarked on a thrilling adventure as a Student Technology Assistant. From September 2021 to December 2021, this role thrust me into the heart of cutting-edge technology and marked my first job in the United States.
As the go-to tech guru on campus, my daily tasks involved breathing life into computer systems. Whether it was orchestrating the symphony of software updates, fine-tuning drive performance for optimal speed, or seamlessly configuring brand-new machines, I was at the forefront of it all.

What made this experience truly exhilarating was the rich tapestry of operating systems I got to master. From the ever-reliable Windows to the world of limitless possibilities offered by Linux and the sleek elegance of MacOS, I navigated them all with finesse. The magic, of course, happened through the ingenious FOG project and a toolkit of advanced tech wizardry.
But my journey at B-CU was more than just honing technical skills. It was a transformative experience that also taught me valuable life skills like teamwork, problem-solving, and adaptability.

A Journey of Innovation at Dell (Summer 2023)

The summer of 2023 was ablaze with possibilities as I embarked on an exhilarating journey as a Hardware/Software Engineer Intern at Dell's renowned headquarters in Hopkinton, Massachusetts. This transformative experience not only deepened my passion for innovation but also allowed me to etch my mark on the fascinating landscape of SSD firmware development.
At the heart of my internship lay the trailblazing domain of Tool Development, a realm that demanded ingenuity, unwavering dedication, and a flair for automation. Armed with Python, I sculpted sophisticated tools that breathed life into the SSD firmware development process. These ingenious creations not only automated critical tasks but also laid the bedrock for the back-end logic within a Linux-based environment.

One of the standout achievements of my internship was the strategic replacement of vendor-centric SAS SSD tools with open-source alternatives. This audacious move not only heightened efficiency but also fortified reliability, setting a new gold standard for SSD development.
But the journey of automation didn't conclude there; I pushed the boundaries further by streamlining the firmware update process. This feat entailed integrating functionalities for various SSD types, with a special focus on SAS and NVMe. The result was a seamlessly automated process, a time-saving marvel that left no room for error.
In my relentless quest for perfection, I pioneered robust error-handling modules that revolutionized troubleshooting. This innovative solution eradicated the laborious manual process, slashing troubleshooting time by over 75%. The impact was nothing short of profound, paving the way for swifter, more efficient resolutions to firmware-related challenges.
